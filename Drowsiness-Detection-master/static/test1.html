DROWSINESS DETECTION



Fatigue behind the wheel can result from various factors, including the use of medication, sleep disorders, or insufficient rest. Recent research highlights the potential dangers associated with drowsy driving. Even missing just one to two
hours of the recommended seven hours of nightly sleep can significantly elevate the risk of a car accident. This underscores the importance of drivers giving priority to ample rest and refraining from driving when experiencing fatigue.


Stay awake, for safety's sake.
